Project Overview

Built a TriScale digital-twin workflow to support synthetic biology design through multiscale modeling from molecular interaction to growth dynamics.

Research Motivation

Experimental iteration in system engineering is costly; modeling-guided strategy can reduce blind testing and improve decision quality.

Methods / Pipeline

  • Integrated molecular docking, AI-guided enzyme redesign, and growth-curve ML models.
  • Predicted PEX5-PTS1 affinities to guide signal peptide selection.
  • Redesigned rate-limiting t-HMGR for improved catalytic stability prediction.
  • Modeled Y. lipolytica growth using Gompertz + Random Forest/XGBoost.

My Contributions

  • Led modeling architecture and integration strategy across submodules.
  • Connected molecular-level predictions with system-level process optimization.
  • Drove quantitative framework for team-level engineering decisions.

Key Outcomes

  • Reduced trial-and-error burden in pathway and condition optimization.
  • Delivered a coherent model-backed design narrative for competition outputs.
  • Contributed to Gold Medal and Best iGemers recognition.

Related Tools / Skills

  • Docking pipelines, ML modeling, growth-curve fitting
  • Cross-team technical leadership and model interpretation
  • Model-to-experiment translation in synthetic biology contexts
