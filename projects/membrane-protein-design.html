Project Gateway 02

De Novo Design of Functional Membrane Proteins

Westlake University · Jun 2025 - Sep 2025 · Research Assistant

AlphaFold2/3 Boltz2 Chai-1 Cryo-EM

Project Overview

Explored a de novo membrane protein design pathway that couples conformational transitions with multifunctional activity through bridge design.

Research Motivation

A central challenge is balancing flexibility and stability while preserving state-specific function in membrane systems.

Methods / Pipeline

  • Compared template-free and template-guided predictions using AF2.3/3, Boltz2, and Chai-1.
  • Used template-based AF3 to stabilize open/closed conformations and generate state seeds.
  • Applied RFdiffusion for helix-domain-bridge assembly and clash-aware candidate filtering.
  • Validated selected candidates through cloning, expression/purification, and cryo-EM preparation.

My Contributions

  • Systematically benchmarked conformation prediction pathways for design-state control.
  • Participated in computational construction of transition-coupled bridge elements.
  • Contributed to wet-lab handoff and structural validation preparation.

Key Outcomes

  • Improved state-specific structural seeds for downstream membrane design.
  • Defined filtering logic to remove lipid-clash and instability-prone candidates.
  • Connected computational proposals to structural validation workflow.

Related Tools / Skills

  • AF2/AF3, Boltz2, Chai-1, RFdiffusion
  • Transmembrane structural reasoning and conformational-state design
  • Cryo-EM sample preparation and structure-focused validation
