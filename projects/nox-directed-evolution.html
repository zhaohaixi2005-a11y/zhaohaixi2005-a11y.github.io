Project Overview

Optimized NADH oxidase systems to enhance catalytic efficiency for sustainable NADH regeneration and improved H2O2 production in lignin-related applications.

Problem Statement

Native NOX variants often show limitations in substrate handling and cofactor interactions, constraining product yield and process robustness.

Methods / Pipeline

  • Screened reaction systems to isolate high-performing NOX isoforms.
  • Used MEGA-X sequence alignment + docking + validation to identify critical residues.
  • Quantified NADH/FAD interaction tendencies with molecular thermodynamic modeling.
  • Ran MD simulation + saturation mutagenesis + directed evolution for optimization.

My Contributions

  • Integrated computational residue-priority analysis with experimental mutation strategy.
  • Participated in cofactor-interface refinement and candidate evaluation loops.
  • Helped frame system-level design goals around catalytic stability and output yield.

Key Outcomes

  • Established a cross-validated optimization workflow for NOX engineering.
  • Improved understanding of cofactor retention determinants for catalytic performance.
  • Produced directed-evolution candidates with improved predicted activity profiles.

Related Tools / Skills

  • MEGA-X, docking suites, MD workflows, mutagenesis planning
  • Sequence-function analysis and cofactor interaction modeling
  • Experimental-computational iteration in enzyme engineering
